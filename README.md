## 📄 Project 2: Food Security and Livelihoods (FSL) - PDM Report

### 1. Executive Summary
This report presents the findings of the Post-Distribution Monitoring (PDM) conducted two weeks after an FSL asset distribution in Khartoum. The objective was to verify field data quality, assess beneficiary satisfaction, and track the initial utilization of distributed livelihood assets against the project's Logframe indicators.

### 2. Methodology & Data Quality
* **Data Collection Tool:** Digital mobile surveys designed via **XLSForm** and deployed using **KoboToolbox**.
* **Sample Size:** 150 randomly selected households out of the reached beneficiaries (95% confidence level).
* **Data Cleaning:** Raw data was extracted from KoboToolbox and cleaned using **Microsoft Excel** to ensure zero duplicate entries and strict data integrity before analysis.

### 3. Key Findings & Indicator Tracking Table (ITT)

| Logframe Indicator | Target | Achieved | Progress (%) | Status |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Number of households receiving seed kits and grants | 500 | 450 | 90% | On Track |
| % of beneficiaries reporting satisfaction with the process | 80% | 85% | 106% | Target Exceeded |
| % of households that successfully resumed production | 75% | 70% | 93% | On Track |

* **Asset Delivery:** 98% of the surveyed households confirmed receiving the items completely and without damage.
* **Utilization:** 70% of the beneficiaries have already planted the seeds and utilized the small grants to repair agricultural tools, directly aligning with the project's outcome-level indicators.

### 4. Accountability & Community Response Mechanism (CRM)
Through the activated CRM channels, 12 feedback inputs were recorded and managed:
* **9 Technical Queries:** Regarding planting timelines and advice (successfully referred to the FSL technical program team).
* **3 Accessibility Complaints:** Regarding physical access constraints to the distribution center due to localized security conditions.

### 5. Challenges & Lessons Learned
* **Challenge:** 10% of the target population (50 households) could not reach the distribution site due to sudden movement restrictions in certain sectors of Khartoum.
* **Lesson Learned / Recommendation:** For future FSL interventions, the MEAL and Program teams recommend establishing **decentralized, smaller distribution points** or collaborating with mobile local partners to deliver assets closer to the beneficiaries' neighborhoods, ensuring safe access and 100% target achievement.
